About the Crop:

Onion (Allium cepa) N-53 matures in about 4 to 5 months from planting. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH of 6.0 to 7.0. Each acre can produce approximately 20 to 30 tons of onions. The crop requires around 500 to 700 mm of water per growing season, with consistent moisture needed for optimal bulb development. Input costs, including seed, fertilizers, irrigation, and labor, typically range from ₹60,000 to ₹90,000 per acre. With market prices ranging from ₹20 to ₹50 per kg, the net profit can range from ₹2,00,000 to ₹10,00,000 per acre, depending on yield and market conditions. Effective management practices, such as proper irrigation and pest control, can enhance both yield and profitability.

  • Soil Composition: Well-Drained Loamy Soil with Ph 6.0 to 7.0
  • Water need per Acre: 500-700 mm per acre annually
  • Potential Dangers: Delia cepae, Peronospora destructor, Phoma terrestris
  • Fertilizers: Ammonium Nitrate (33-0-0), Muriate of Potash, Zinc Sulfate
  • Pesticides: Diazinon, Bacillus thuringiensis, Fosetyl-Al (Aliette)
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹2,90,000 to ₹14,25,000 per acre annually
